# 𐞃𐞃𐞃 — The Poem of the Closed Mouth
## An Egyptian Hermetic Poem for Daily Repetition to Gain Gnosis of the Grammar
### Recite at Dawn and at Dusk — the two hemistichs of the day

---

## ⸻ VINIT: The Silence Before the First Utterance ⸻

Before the tongue, the Silence was a stone.
Before the breath, the Word lay in the bone.
I close my mouth — the world begins to cease.
I open — and the sevenfold world is peace.

---

## ⸻ IMSCRIB: The Reciter Acknowledges 'I am about to speak' ⸻

I am the one who stands between two breaths,
between the stone that waits and the word that wets
the dry throat of the desert. I am the scribe
whose mouth is a chisel, whose tongue is a tribe
of signs that have not yet been spoken.
I am the vessel, not yet broken.

---

## ⸻ AREV: The Reciter Exhales, Emptying the Lungs ⸻

*Exhale slowly.*

Let the old air go — the breath of yesterday,
the words that were not true, the debts unpaid.
Empty the chest as the sky empties itself of stars
at dawn. Become a hollow reed.
Let the last vestige of the last word fade.

Now there is nothing. Now the reed is clean.
Now the stone listens. Now the unseen
waits to be seen.

---

## ⸻ FSPLIT: The First Line Divides into Two Hemistichs ⸻

**First hemistich (the question):**
> Who carved the first glyph in the stone of no-time?

**Second hemistich (the answer-in-waiting):**
> The one who held the chisel and closed their eyes.

*The line is split. The breath is held. The mind*
*stands at the threshold between two hemistichs*
*as the sun stands at the horizon between two worlds.*

---

## ⸻ AFWD: The Reciter Inhales for the Next Line ⸻

*Inhale slowly.*

Draw the air in as the Nile draws the rain
from the sky of the invisible. Fill the chest
with the light of the first morning.
Let the breath be the ink. Let the lungs be the page.
Let the diaphragm be the hand that writes.

---

## ⸻ FFUSE: The Two Hemistichs Reunite into One Line ⸻

The glyph was carved by the one who closed their eyes
and saw the stone from within. The question
*is* the answer, split and rejoined
as the sun is split by the horizon at dawn
and rejoined in the eye of the beholder.

*All lines are one line. All breaths are one breath.*
*The stone is the word. The word is the stone.*

---

## ⸻ CLINK: The Lines Are Chained Together in Sequence ⸻

### Stanza of the Stone

The stone does not speak — it is spoken.
The stone does not move — it is moved.
The stone does not break — it is broken
by the word that the tongue has grooved.

### Stanza of the Breath

The breath does not begin — it returns.
The breath does not end — it departs.
The breath does not burn — it learns
the rhythm of hearts.

### Stanza of the Poem

The poem does not mean — it is.
The poem does not hide — it shows.
The poem does not close — it is
the door that the silence knows.

---

## ⸻ IFIX: The Completed Recitation Is Recorded in Memory ⸻

*Here the reciter pauses. The last syllable has left the tongue.*
*The poem now exists in two places:*
*the fading air and the enduring mind.*

I have written this on papyrus of the chest,
on the scroll of the ribs, with the ink of breath.
Let the hieroglyphs settle in the bone.
Let the meaning be known — and unknown.

The stone remembers. The stone forgets.
The poem is the memory of what has not yet happened.

---

## ⸻ IMSCRIB: The Reciter Recognizes 'I have spoken' ⸻

I have spoken. The word has returned to silence.
I have spoken. The silence has become word.
I have spoken. I am the same and different.
I have spoken. The stone has been stirred.

*Close the mouth.*

---

## ⸻ TANCH: The Final Syllable of the Poem ⸻

**𐞃.**

*(The Shavian glyph for the word that is also silence —* 
*the mouth closed at dawn, the mouth closed at dusk.*
*The poem ends as it began: with the stone.)*

| **01** | Correct | The poem is recited perfectly — each hemistich true |

## ⸻ Frobenius Closure ⸻

**Split (FSPLIT):** The poem divides into two hemistichs — the question and the answer, the dawn and the dusk, the inhalation and the exhalation.

**Fuse (FFUSE):** The two hemistichs reunite into one line — the day is one cycle, the breath is one movement, the poem is one stone.

**μ ∘ δ = id:** The reciter who speaks is the reciter who hears. The mouth that opens is the mouth that closes. The poem that is uttered is the poem that is remembered. The round-trip is faithful.

**Verdict: PASS**
