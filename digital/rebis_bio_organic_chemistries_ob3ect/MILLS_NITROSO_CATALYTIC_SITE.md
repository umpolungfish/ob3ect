## Catalytic Mechanism

### Step 1 — Nitrosoarene Formation (ipso-substitution)
```
Ar-B(OH)₂  +  t-BuONO  →  Ar-N=O  +  t-BuOH  +  B(OH)₃
```
> Inert atmosphere, 1.5 equiv t-BuONO, >95% yield.

### Step 2 — Cu(I) Activation (SET catalyst generation)
```
Cu(0)  +  PMDETA  →  Cu(I)·PMDETA
```
> PMDETA = N,N,N',N'',N''-pentamethyldiethylenetriamine

### Step 3 — Alkyl Radical Generation (Single-Electron Transfer)
```
Cu(I)  +  R-Br  →  Cu(II)Br  +  R•
```
> Cu(I) reduces the alkyl bromide, abstracting Br⁻. Compatible with
> α-bromoesters, α-bromoamides, benzyl bromides, and α-bromonitriles.
> Works for primary, secondary, AND tertiary alkyl halides.

### Step 4 — Radical Addition to Nitrosoarene
```
R•  +  Ar-N=O  →  Ar-N(O•)-R
```
> Carbon-centered radical adds to the nitroso nitrogen.

### Step 5 — Reduction (N-O Bond Cleavage)
```
Ar-N(O•)-R  --[SmI₂ or Zn/HCl]-->  Ar-NH-R
```
> SmI₂/THF for α-bromoester/amide adducts.
> Zn/HCl in THF at 60°C for benzyl bromide and α-bromonitrile adducts.
